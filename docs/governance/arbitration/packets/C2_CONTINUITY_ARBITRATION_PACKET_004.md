## 2. Packet Subject

### Arbitration Question

```
How should the repository distinguish:
  - Topology (structural relationships)
  - Continuity (transition behavior)

And how should continuity be decomposed into:
  - Geometric continuity (junction quality)
  - Manufacturing continuity (production constraints)
  - Shell continuity (structural coherence)
  - Runtime continuity (execution state)
  - Semantic continuity (meaning preservation)
```

### Why This Matters

C2-B discovered continuity cannot remain embedded inside topology.
C2-D/C2-DP formally decomposes continuity as an independent semantic surface.

Without continuity decomposition:
- Topology and continuity collapse
- Manufacturing constraints become ontology
- Runtime assumptions become governance
- Semantic meaning drifts silently

### Core Discovery

```
Continuity is emerging as a semantic transition discipline
rather than merely a geometry property.
```

## 3. Foundational Distinction

### Topology vs Continuity

```
Topology describes structure.
Continuity describes transition behavior.
```

| Concept | Core Question |
|---------|---------------|
| surface_topology | How do surfaces relate/connect? |
| manufacturing_continuity | Are transitions operationally manufacturable? |

These may correlate. They are NOT equivalent.

### Non-Equivalence Proof

**Example 1:**
```
Topology relationship exists (surfaces connect)
Continuity fails (junction too sharp for manufacturing)
```

**Example 2:**
```
Manufacturing continuity constraint applies
Regardless of topology representation (flat_extrusion or lofted)
```
