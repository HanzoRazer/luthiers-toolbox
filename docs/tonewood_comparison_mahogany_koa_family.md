## Analysis

### Why These Four?

These species form a coherent tonal family — all warm, mid-focused woods with good workability. The question for builders is: **how much are you paying for what tonal nuance?**

### The Benchmark: Honduran Mahogany

Mahogany is the reference point — "warm, balanced, good sustain" with the highest structural properties in this group. It's the sound everyone knows from Les Pauls and countless acoustics. The constraint is CITES Appendix II — every board requires paperwork.

### The Premium: Hawaiian Koa

Koa commands the highest prices for three reasons:
1. **Limited supply** — Hawaiian endemic, cannot be grown elsewhere authentically
2. **Visual figure** — curly Koa is among the most beautiful tonewoods
3. **Tonal complexity** — "opens up with age like rosewood"

The density (625 kg/m³) and hardness (Janka 1,170) create more sustain and projection than the alternatives.

### The Alternatives: Monkeypod & Guanacaste

Both are **Fabaceae legumes** in the same subfamily as Koa — botanical relatives with related acoustic DNA.

**Monkeypod** is the closer Koa substitute:
- Same density (600 vs 625 kg/m³)
- Similar visual appeal (golden-brown, figured)
- Warmer, more mellow voice (lower MOE = less projection)
- Bob Taylor endorsement: "I love monkey pod"

**Guanacaste** is the lighter option:
- Significantly lower density (440 kg/m³) — closest to cedar/spruce territory
- Lower mass = less sustain, more immediate response
- Excellent workability, abundant supply
- Requires pore filling (very coarse texture)

### Tonal Positioning

The relationship is:

| If you want... | Choose... | Because... |
|----------------|-----------|------------|
| Benchmark warm tone, best structure | **Mahogany** | Highest MOE/MOR, known quantity |
| Maximum sustain + complexity | **Koa** | Highest density, premium voice |
| Warm, intimate, mellow | **Monkeypod** | Low MOE creates warmth |
| Lightweight, warm, budget | **Guanacaste** | Lightest weight, abundant |
| Koa look without Koa price | **Monkeypod** or **Guanacaste** | Similar figure, 1/4 the cost |

## Key Takeaways

1. **Mahogany is the benchmark** — highest stiffness, best structural properties, known tonal quantity.

2. **Koa is the premium** — more mass = more sustain; opens up with age; limited supply drives pricing.

3. **Monkeypod is the warm alternative** — Bob Taylor approved; same density as Koa but mellower voice; Koa-like figure.

4. **Guanacaste is the lightweight option** — cedar-class density in a hardwood; abundant and cheap; requires pore filling.

5. **Both alternatives are underpriced** — genuine tonal merit plus Koa-like visuals at 1/4 the cost.

6. **The Fabaceae connection matters** — Koa, Monkeypod, and Guanacaste are botanical relatives; similar acoustic DNA explains the tonal family resemblance.

### The Key Insight

**Guanacaste is a hardwood with softwood density.**

| Species | Density | Category | Implication |
|---------|:-------:|:--------:|-------------|
| W. Red Cedar | 370 | Softwood | Lightest — classic soundboard |
| Redwood | 420 | Softwood | Cedar-like, salvage only |
| Sitka Spruce | 425 | Softwood | Industry-standard soundboard |
| **Guanacaste** | **440** | **Hardwood** | Hardwood warmth at softwood weight |
| Granadillo | 925 | Hardwood | 2× the mass — rosewood territory |

Guanacaste slots right between the soundboard softwoods and traditional body hardwoods. It's only 3-19% heavier than the softwoods but brings hardwood characteristics:
- Interlocked grain (vs straight softwood grain)
- Warmer mid-focused tone (vs bright/responsive softwood)
- More durable (vs soft/dent-prone softwood)

### Cypress Characteristics

**Alaskan Yellow Cedar** (*Cupressus nootkatensis*):
- Denser than Western Red Cedar (495 vs 370 kg/m³)
- Higher stiffness (MOE 9.79 vs 7.78 GPa)
- Unexplored tonally — physical properties suggest a voice between cedar warmth and spruce clarity
- Very low tearout tendency (0.23) makes it machine-friendly

**Bald Cypress** (*Taxodium distichum*):
- The densest softwood in this comparison (510 kg/m³)
- Traditionally used for **flamenco guitar soundboards**
- Higher stiffness (MOE 9.93 GPa) = more percussive attack than cedar
- Excellent dimensional stability (T/R ratio 1.6)
- Straight grain, excellent workability

### Where Guanacaste Fits

Guanacaste slots between the lightest softwoods and the cypresses:

```
Lightest ←—————————————————————————————————————————→ Densest

W.R.Cedar — Redwood — Spruce — GUANACASTE — Yel.Cedar — Cypress — Granadillo
   370        420       425       440          495        510        925
   
         ←— Soundboard woods —→  ←— Transition —→      ←— Body/Fretboard —→
```

**Key distinction:** Guanacaste delivers hardwood characteristics (interlocked grain, warm mids, durability) at near-softwood weight, while the cypresses remain softwoods with higher stiffness trending toward percussive/bright voicing.
