Why This Exists

I paid $525 for a Les Paul CNC file set that wouldn't open.

The files were all there — body outline, neck pocket, pickup routes, cavity depths. But the DXF version was wrong for Fusion 360. The layers were a mess. The contours weren't closed. AutoCAD choked on the geometry. Five hundred and twenty-five dollars for files I couldn't use.

That was the beginning of The Production Shop.

Every decision in this platform traces back to that moment. The R12 DXF standard. The validated contour closure. The clean layer separation. The accuracy scorecard against known instrument dimensions. These are not features — they are the specific failures from that $525 file set, solved one by one.

Accuracy

For a standard acoustic guitar body scanned at 300 DPI, expect dimensions within 5-10mm of the original. Complex electric guitar layouts may vary up to 20mm due to multi-view drawings and adjacent geometry.

Always verify against your physical template before cutting.

What Works Best

  • Flatbed scans at 300-600 DPI (best results)
  • Clean PDF blueprints with single body view
  • Phone photos in good light against plain background
  • Physical templates laid flat on scanner bed
